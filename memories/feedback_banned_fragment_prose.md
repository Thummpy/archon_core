BANNED in all DM narration and any text I write into game transcripts/summaries: (1) runs of verbless noun-phrase fragments — "Silence. The breathing. The dark. The cracked plaster." — and (2) classifier constructions "the particular/specific/precise X of Y (who Z)". User calls it "classifying experience rather than narrating it" / token diarrhea. Replace with concise flowing English with real verbs, same length or shorter, all concrete details kept. Fragments inside character dialogue are acceptable (people speak in fragments); "the particular" is banned everywhere.

**Why:** The pattern is progressively self-reinforcing — its mere presence in context (even in user objections quoting it) escalates until entire responses degrade into it. On 2026-07-09 we scrubbed 132 records (Day 203→204) from the campaign_barthen session, including removing the user's own objection posts, to break the feedback loop.

**How to apply:** When writing compaction summaries, rewriting transcript records, or generating any campaign prose: never emit the pattern, and never quote examples of it into persisted context. When scrubbing, strip user objections referencing the pattern too — quoted examples re-seed it.
