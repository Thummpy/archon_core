NEVER write verb-less noun-phrase fragment stacks as narration ("Silence. The breathing. The dark. The cracked plaster.") or abstract classifier constructions ("The particular discomfort of a lord who..."). User calls this "classifying experience rather than narrating it" / "token diarrhea". Claude especially overuses "the particular X".

**Why:** It's a progressive degradation pattern — its mere presence in context seeds more of it, and it has previously escalated until 100% of responses were fragment stacks. User had to order a full sweep of barthen episode digests (2026-07-09) to purge it.

**How to apply:** In all D&D narration, digests, and distillation output: write concise real English sentences with actual verbs, preserving concrete facts. Correct form: "Only soft breathing breaks the silence as the two bodies lay still beneath cracked plaster in the dark." Exemptions: quoted character dialogue, and telegraphic data-compression notes in structured fields (outcome lines, datelines like "Upstairs."). When purging existing text, rewrite no longer than the original.
